Oakville Neighbourhoods We Serve

Seven Stones Landscape delivers premium hardscaping and landscape design across every Oakville neighbourhood. Oakville has some of the GTA's most demanding residential landscape standards, and our work is engineered to match — proper 8 to 10 inch compacted bases, premium materials, heritage-aware design, and mature-tree protection from day one.

Old Oakville and Morrison: Heritage Estate Properties

Old Oakville and Morrison hold some of the oldest and most valuable estate properties in the GTA, with heritage-designated homes, 80+ year old tree canopy, and strict Heritage Conservation District guidelines. Projects require Town of Oakville Heritage Permit review (4 to 10 weeks) and typically use natural Wiarton limestone, Indiana limestone, Techo-Bloc Aberdeen, and Unilock Courtstone for heritage compatibility. Estate-scale projects here commonly run $150,000 to $650,000.

Eastlake: Large-Lot Lakefront Estates

Eastlake has Oakville's largest estate lots and lakefront properties. Projects almost always involve pool surrounds, multi-level terraces, outdoor kitchens, fire features, and integrated landscape lighting. Conservation Halton regulates shoreline work within 30 metres of Lake Ontario — typical permit review is 6 to 12 weeks. We've delivered comprehensive Eastlake landscape redesigns in the $350,000 to $1.5M range coordinated with pool builders, licensed electricians, irrigation specialists, and arborists.

Glen Abbey and Bronte Creek: Golf-Course Community Homes

Glen Abbey's homes around the Glen Abbey Golf Club and Bronte Creek Provincial Park properties combine established landscape standards with ravine-proximity considerations. Homes backing Bronte Creek or Fourteen Mile Creek need Conservation Halton permits for hardscape and grading within regulated zones. Soil is a mix of clay and glacial gravel that varies block-to-block; we verify conditions during quoting.

Bronte Village: Lakefront and Waterfront Properties

Bronte Village lakefront properties have Conservation Halton jurisdiction and often erosion-protection requirements. Bronte inland homes are a mix of older heritage cottages on smaller lots and newer infill builds. Material choices favour natural stone and premium pavers to match the village character.

Joshua Creek and Iroquois Ridge North

Joshua Creek and Iroquois Ridge North have large executive homes on 60 to 100-foot frontage lots. Many properties back onto ravines (Joshua Creek, Sixteen Mile Creek) requiring Conservation Halton permits for near-ravine work. Heavy clay subgrade requires 8 to 10 inch compacted base specifications. Typical backyard projects run $55,000 to $185,000.

West Oak Trails, River Oaks, Uptown Core: Newer Executive Homes

The newer north Oakville subdivisions sit on heavily-compacted clay. Lot-grading plans are on file with the town and must be respected. Many 5 to 15 year old homes need drainage correction post-construction, typically French drains, swales, and catch basins. Front-yard curb appeal upgrades with premium interlock driveways are the most requested service here.

Palermo, Clearview, and Rural Oakville

Rural and semi-rural Oakville properties north of Dundas Street often have wells and septic systems with specific setbacks. We verify well (30 m setback from hardscape drainage) and septic field records during quoting and adapt design accordingly.

Every Oakville project starts with a free on-site consultation, confirmed permit and tree-protection requirements, and a written fixed-price scope. We coordinate heritage review, Conservation Halton permits, arborist reports, and geotechnical input when the site requires it. Request a free Oakville quote or call (289) 700-0312.
