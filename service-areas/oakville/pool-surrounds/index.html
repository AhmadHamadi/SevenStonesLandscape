Pool Surrounds and Pool Decks in Oakville

By Riaad, Owner & Project Manager · ICPI Certified Installer · Last updated May 19, 2026

Oakville has more pools per capita than almost any other Halton community, and the pool surround is what makes the backyard usable for the other 22 hours of the day when no one is swimming. We design and build pool decks across Oakville with the right slip-resistant material, drainage planning, and fence integration to meet Halton Region pool bylaws.

Quick answer: Seven Stones Landscape installs pool surrounds across Oakville — Eastlake, Joshua Creek and nearby — for $35,000 to $75,000 on a standard 600 to 1,000 sq ft in-ground deck (2026), built on a compacted, drainage-first base with 2 percent cross-slope. ICPI-certified, $5M insured.

Why Oakville Homeowners Hire Us

A pool surround is more than the pavers immediately next to the water. It is the deck, the seating area, the path to the gate, the drainage, and the way the whole back of the lot reads as one space. We scope Oakville pool projects with all of that in mind, not just the strip of pavers around the coping.

Halton Region requires a 4-foot minimum pool fence with a self-closing gate. Material selection has to handle wet feet, sunscreen, chlorine, and the freeze-thaw cycle. We recommend Unilock ArmourCore, Techo-Bloc Blu 60 or Industria Smooth for most pool surrounds because they have a slip-resistant finish, are stocked locally, and individual pavers can be lifted later if the pool needs service.

Pool projects often involve seat walls and elevation changes, grading and drainage away from the pool basin, and backyard landscaping that integrates with the deck. We can scope the surround alone, or combine it with the rest of the backyard build if you are doing a phased project.

Local Considerations & Credentials

  • Credentials: ICPI certified, Landscape Ontario member, authorized Unilock and Techo-Bloc installer, $5M liability, full WSIB. Familiar with Halton Region pool enclosure bylaws and self-closing gate requirements.
  • Site Conditions: Most Oakville pool installs are on clay-rich Halton Till. Pool excavation creates compacted disturbed soil around the pool that needs careful base prep before pavers. Eastlake lakefront properties may need Conservation Halton review if work falls within 30 metres of Lake Ontario.
  • Performance Detail: Cross-slope of 2 percent away from the pool basin, slip-resistant paver finish, polymeric sand specifically rated for pool environments, and either an aluminum edge restraint or a poured concrete bond beam at the coping. Drainage daylighted to a safe location, never to the neighbour.
  • Local Tip: Pool surround installation often happens in the same season as the pool itself. We coordinate with most Oakville pool installers (CGT, Discovery, Solda, etc.) so the pool deck can be installed within 2 to 6 weeks of pool completion. Plan ahead: peak-season installs book out 8 to 16 weeks.

Why Oakville Homeowners Choose Seven Stones for Pool Surrounds

Choosing a pool surround contractor in Oakville is really about who understands what happens after the pool is dug. The disturbed, clay-rich Halton Till left around a fresh excavation settles unevenly, and a deck built on it without a proper drainage-first base will move within a few seasons. As an ICPI-certified pool surround company established in 2013, we build the base to handle that settlement and the freeze-thaw cycle, whether the lot is a flat build in Joshua Creek, a sloped property near Bronte, or a lakefront home in Eastlake that may fall under Conservation Halton's 30-metre review.

Because we are an authorized Unilock and Techo-Bloc installer, you get slip-rated pavers matched to wet feet and chlorine, all backed by $5M liability coverage, a 5-year workmanship warranty, and a fixed written quote with no surprises. Oakville Pool Surrounds FAQ

A standard Oakville pool surround (600 to 1,000 sq ft of paving around an in-ground pool) using Unilock ArmourCore or Techo-Bloc Blu 60 with proper drainage and edge restraint runs $35,000 to $75,000. Estate-level surrounds with seat walls, fire features, integrated lighting, and premium pavers commonly run $95,000 to $200,000. Pool deck only, fence and pool basin not included.
For pool decks specifically: Unilock ArmourCore, Techo-Bloc Blu 60 Smooth, Industria Smooth, and Para LineaRoc are the four we install most often. They have slip-resistant finishes, hold up to chlorine and salt, and stay cool underfoot in summer. Avoid sealed travertine and polished natural stone for any wet-foot surface; they get slick within 2 to 3 seasons.
The pool itself needs a building permit and pool fence enclosure approval. The surround paving inside the fence does not need a separate permit if it stays at grade. If a seat wall or retaining wall on the surround is over 1 metre exposed height, that wall needs a permit. Halton Region pool bylaw requires the fence to be in place before the pool is filled.
Two layers: the paver surface itself sheds water through the joints, and we set the surround with a 1.5 to 2 percent cross-slope away from the pool basin and away from the house. Where ground slopes back toward the pool we add catch basins and either a French drain or a connection to the existing storm system. We never daylight pool drainage onto a neighbouring property.
A 700 sq ft Oakville pool surround takes 6 to 12 working days: 2 days excavation and grading, 1 to 2 days base install and compaction, 3 to 5 days paving, 1 to 2 days polymeric sand, edge restraint, and finish. Larger surrounds with seat walls, lighting, and outdoor kitchen prep run 3 to 6 weeks.

Every Seven Stones pool surround in Oakville carries our 5-year workmanship warranty covering base settlement, edge restraint failure, and joint or coping issues caused by installation. Unilock ArmourCore and Techo-Bloc pavers carry their own manufacturer guarantees against structural failure. The warranty assumes the pool deck is not undermined by later pool-basin leaks or third-party excavation, which sit outside our scope.
Pool decks need slightly more upkeep than a dry patio because of constant splash-out and chlorine. Rinse off sunscreen and pool chemicals, top up polymeric sand in the joints every 2 to 4 years, and re-seal with a pool-rated penetrating sealer every 3 to 5 years to resist staining. Sweep leaf litter off promptly in fall so tannins from Oakville's mature maples do not mark light pavers.

The key is our drainage-first base on Halton Till clay, which sheds meltwater before it can freeze under the deck. Use a plastic shovel and sand, never rock salt or de-icer, on the surround through winter to protect the paver finish.
Deck square footage and paver line drive the base price, but the biggest swing factors are seat walls, integrated lighting, and grade changes. A flat Joshua Creek lot paves quickly, while a sloped Eastlake or Bronte lot needing retaining walls and extra drainage adds cost. Premium coping, fire features, and outdoor-kitchen prep are what push a surround from the $35,000 to $75,000 range toward estate-level pricing.
