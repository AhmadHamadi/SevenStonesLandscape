Interlock Driveway Installation in Mississauga

By Riaad, Owner & Project Manager · ICPI Certified Installer · Last updated May 19, 2026

If you are searching for an interlock driveway contractor in Mississauga, you have probably already noticed how varied the housing stock is here. Lorne Park and Mineola estate lots, Port Credit character homes, and Erin Mills and Churchill Meadows executive subdivisions all have different driveway expectations. We build paver driveways across every Mississauga neighbourhood, and the spec changes with the lot.

ICPI-certified, $5M insured, 5-year workmanship warranty.

Why Mississauga Homeowners Hire Us

Mississauga driveways span a wide range. Many Lorne Park and Mineola properties have circular drives, double-wide lay-bys, or matched aprons that connect to street paving, while the executive subdivisions in Erin Mills and Churchill Meadows favour clean double-wide drives. That kind of work is not a copy-paste install. The base depth, edge restraint, and drainage cross-slope all need to be designed for the actual lot before any pavers go down.

Older streets in Port Credit and Streetsville have their own street-facing material expectations. Homes near the Credit River and the lake often need pavers that complement a mature streetscape and read well from the curb. We work with Unilock Il Campo, Techo-Bloc Industria, and natural cobble for those projects.

Many Mississauga driveways pair well with new concrete steps, front-yard landscaping, and flagstone walkways. If the front yard is being planned as one project rather than several, we can scope it that way from the first quote.

Local Considerations & Credentials

  • Credentials: ICPI certified, Landscape Ontario member, authorized Unilock and Techo-Bloc installer, $5M liability, $10M umbrella for higher-value Mississauga estate work, full WSIB. Est. 2013. 5-year workmanship warranty.
  • Site Conditions: Most Mississauga lots sit on Peel Plain clay. Lots near the lake in Port Credit and Lakeview have sandier, lower-lying soil. Ravine lots along the Credit River in Lorne Park and Mineola need careful grading, and newer subdivisions in the west (Churchill Meadows, Lisgar) have heavily-compacted clay subgrades from construction that need aeration before base install.
  • Performance Detail: Standard Mississauga driveway base is 8 inches of compacted 3/4-clear limestone. Estate driveways with frequent heavy vehicle traffic (trades, deliveries) get 10 to 12 inches. Polymeric sand and aluminum or concrete edge restraint on all exposed edges, every project.
  • Local Tip: Mississauga requires a road-occupancy or driveway-entrance permit any time the curb cut, boulevard, or apron is altered, and the Private Tree Protection By-law can apply when a regulated tree is near the work. On ravine lots, Credit Valley Conservation governs work in the west and central city, while the Toronto and Region Conservation Authority (TRCA) covers eastern Mississauga along Etobicoke Creek and Lakeview. We confirm jurisdiction and pull the permit as part of the project.

Mississauga Interlock Driveways FAQ

A 2-car Mississauga interlock driveway (450 to 650 sq ft) with standard Unilock or Techo-Bloc pavers, 8-inch compacted base, polymeric sand and edge restraint runs $32,000 to $55,000. Lorne Park and Mineola premium driveways using Beacon Hill Flagstone, Richcliff, or natural cobble with decorative banding commonly run $65,000 to $135,000. Circular drives and estate aprons are quoted separately.
A road-occupancy or driveway-entrance permit is required from the City of Mississauga whenever the curb cut, boulevard, or apron is changed: widening, replacing the apron, or changing the approach angle. The paver surface inside your property line does not need a building permit. If a regulated tree is near the work, the Mississauga Private Tree Protection By-law may also apply.
For most Mississauga driveways: Unilock Beacon Hill, Il Campo, or Techo-Bloc Industria and Blu 60 are reliable, well-stocked, and repairable. For character streets in Port Credit and Streetsville: Unilock Il Campo or Techo-Bloc Industria blend with the older streetscape. For Lorne Park estate driveways: Unilock Richcliff, Techo-Bloc Blu 60, or natural cobble give the highest curb-appeal lift and the best resale return.
A standard 500 sq ft Mississauga driveway takes 5 to 9 working days: 2 days excavation and disposal, 1 day base install and compaction, 2 to 4 days paving, 1 day polymeric sand and edge restraint. Estate driveways with circular drives, banding, or matched aprons run 2 to 4 weeks. Tight Mineola and Port Credit lots add a few days for material staging.

Every Mississauga driveway carries our 5-year workmanship warranty covering base settlement, edge-restraint failure, and significant lippage from installation faults. Normal wear, road-salt etching, and frost heave from a homeowner-altered grade are excluded. We are $5M insured with a $10M umbrella on estate work.
Plan on topping up polymeric sand in the joints every 2 to 4 years and sealing every 3 to 5 years, sooner on a south-facing Lorne Park drive that gets full sun. Rinse off road salt in late winter, pull any joint weeds early, and re-level any pavers that settle. We offer a paid maintenance visit, and joint and seal work is far cheaper than a full rebuild.

Unilock and Techo-Bloc driveway pavers are rated for de-icing salt. We still recommend rinsing salt off in spring to protect the surface texture and colour.
A properly built Mississauga interlock driveway lasts 30 to 40 years or more, well beyond poured concrete, which usually cracks within 15 to 25 years on local clay. Lifespan depends almost entirely on the base: our 8-inch compacted clear-stone base on aerated Peel Plain clay subgrade is what prevents the settlement that ages a driveway. Individual pavers can be lifted and reset if ever needed.
