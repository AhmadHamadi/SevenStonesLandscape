Common questions about sod installation in Hamilton, Burlington and Oakville.

In 2026, professional sod installation in Hamilton, Burlington, and Oakville typically runs $1.80 to $2.75 per square foot installed, including sod, delivery, and labour. If the yard needs grading and topsoil, add roughly $0.80 to $1.50 per square foot for 3 to 4 inches of triple-mix topsoil. A standard 1,500 sq ft front lawn usually lands around $3,500 to $5,500 complete.
We supply locally-grown Kentucky bluegrass blend sod from Zander Sod Co-Op and Greenhorizons Group, both Ontario growers. Kentucky bluegrass handles Hamilton and Halton winters, recovers well from foot traffic, and greens up in early spring. For heavily shaded yards we can switch to a fescue-bluegrass blend.
The two prime windows in Southern Ontario are mid-April through early June and mid-August through mid-October, when soil temperatures are 10 to 20 C. We install through summer with careful watering, but we avoid laying sod during July heat waves if the client cannot water twice daily. Sod harvest stops once ground freezes, usually mid-November.

Hamilton-area clay subgrade usually needs extra topsoil to establish a healthy root zone. Grading and sod can be quoted as a package.
For the first 14 days, new sod needs 1 inch of water daily, applied in two waterings (early morning and late afternoon) during hot stretches. Weeks 3 and 4, reduce to every other day. By week 5 to 6 the roots are established and normal weekly watering suffices. Skipping early watering is the top cause of sod failure in Burlington and Oakville.
