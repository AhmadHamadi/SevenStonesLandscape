Concrete FAQs

Quick answers to what homeowners ask before booking.

How much does a concrete driveway cost?
In 2026, residential concrete driveways in Hamilton, Burlington and Oakville typically run $14-$22 per square foot for a standard broom-finish pour, including 5 inches of 4000 PSI air-entrained concrete over a 6-inch compacted base. Stamped or exposed aggregate adds roughly 25-40%. Free written quote on every project.
Do you replace old driveways?
Yes, tear-out, base evaluation and rebuild, rebar/mesh reinforcement, then a fresh 5-inch 4000 PSI pour with saw-cut control joints. Most replacements take 3-5 working days from demo to drive-on (28-day full cure for heavy use).
Do you install stamped concrete patios?
Yes, ashlar slate, London cobble, clean slate and flagstone patterns with integral colour and antiquing release. Two coats of solvent-based acrylic cure-and-seal at install.
Is stamped concrete good for Ontario winters?
Yes when built right. 4000 PSI air-entrained concrete, saw-cut control joints on a 10x10 ft grid within 24 hours of pour, and a proper 6-inch granular base. Reseal every 2-3 years to protect the colour from UV and salt.
Do you install concrete walkways and steps?
Yes, OBC-code front steps (7-7/8" max rise, 8-1/4" min run) reinforced with #4 rebar tied into the foundation, plus broom-finish walkways for winter traction.
Do you install backyard concrete pads?
Yes, pads for hot tubs, BBQ areas, sheds, gazebos and seating spaces. Hot-tub pads use 6 inches with a heavier rebar grid; shed pads typically 4 inches with wire mesh.
What areas do you serve?
Hamilton, Burlington, Oakville, Waterdown, Ancaster, Dundas, Stoney Creek, Milton and surrounding Halton and Hamilton-area neighbourhoods.
Can I send photos before booking a quote?
Yes, there's an optional photo upload on the form. Send wide shots plus close-ups of cracks or problem spots and we'll scope the project before the on-site visit.
Do you charge for the on-site quote?
No. Every concrete on-site quote is free with no obligation. We measure the area, check subgrade and access, and walk you through finish options on the spot. Written itemized quote follows within 3 business days.
How soon can you start a concrete project?
Spring and early summer fill 4-8 weeks out across Hamilton, Burlington and Oakville. Smaller projects (steps, walkways, single backyard pad) can sometimes slot in within 2 weeks. Late-season pours through October are typically faster to book.
Do you remove the old driveway or asphalt before pouring?
Yes. Demo and disposal are line items on every quote, we tear out the existing concrete or asphalt, evaluate the granular base, and rebuild it to spec before forming. Reusing a poor base is the #1 reason driveways crack within 5 years.
Do you require a deposit?
Yes, a small project deposit secures your install date and covers materials we order in advance. Balance is typically split between mid-project and post-install once you've signed off on the final walkthrough. All terms are in writing on the quote.
What happens if it rains the day of the pour?
We monitor the forecast for 72 hours pre-pour. Light rain after initial set is fine; heavy rain in the first 4 hours can damage the surface, so we'll reschedule before pour day if the forecast says so. No charge for weather reschedules.
Do you have references or recent projects in my city I can see?
Yes. We share recent projects in Hamilton, Burlington, Oakville, Waterdown, Ancaster, Dundas and Milton on request, many homeowners want to drive past a finished driveway or stamped patio in their neighbourhood before committing. Just ask on the quote call.
