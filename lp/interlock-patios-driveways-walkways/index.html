Why Homeowners Choose Seven Stones

If a paver project fails in Ontario, the answer is almost always under the pavers, not on top. Here's how we build for it.

  • Proper base preparation. 10 to 12 inches of compacted 3/4-clear stone with a Mirafi geotextile separator on clay subgrades.
  • Drainage & grading. Patios pitched to shed water away from the house, not toward it.
  • Rigid edge restraint. Plastic edge with 10-inch spikes into compacted base — not a glued bead of mortar.
  • Clean cuts & finishing. Wet-saw cuts at borders and curves; mitred corners on banded patios.
  • Freeze-thaw durability. Polymeric sand activated correctly so joints don't wash out winter one.
  • Local crew, local service area. Halton- and Hamilton-based since 2013, not a subbed-out operation.
  • Real project photos. Every job documented; we'll show finished work in your neighbourhood.
  • Free written quotes. Itemised so you can compare apples to apples — not a single lump sum.
  • Licensed & insured. $5M general liability and full WSIB coverage.
  • Workmanship warranty. 5-year written workmanship warranty on every interlock project.
  • ICPI certified. Verified hardscape installer credentials — the only third-party install certification recognised in Canada.
  • Unilock & Techo-Bloc authorized. Direct manufacturer support and warranty on every premium paver we install.

Interlock & Stonework FAQs

Quick answers to what homeowners ask before booking.

How much does an interlock patio cost?
In 2026, mid-range interlock patios in Hamilton, Burlington and Oakville run about $24–$38 per square foot installed, including a 10–12-inch compacted base, edge restraint, and polymeric sand. Premium Unilock or Techo-Bloc large-format slabs with banded borders sit higher.
Do you install interlock driveways?
Yes — paver driveway installation with 12-inch compacted base on Halton clay, Unilock or Techo-Bloc driveway-rated pavers, rigid edge restraint, and polymeric sand. ICPI-certified installs.
Do you build backyard paver patios?
Yes — paver patios for outdoor living, BBQ areas, entertaining spaces, fire-pit features, poolside, and full backyard upgrades. Custom layouts with banded borders, mitred cuts and integrated seat walls when called for.
Do you install front entrances and walkways?
Yes — stone walkways, interlock walkways, paver front entrances, steps, side-yard paths and entrance upgrades. We design them to integrate with existing driveways and porches.
Is interlock good for Ontario winters?
Yes — better than concrete in many cases. Pavers flex with frost heave, and damaged units can be lifted and reset. Key is a 10–12-inch compacted base on Halton-area clay and proper edge restraint. Built right, an interlock patio lasts 25+ winters.
Do you install retaining walls?
Yes — segmental block walls (Unilock Pisa2, Techo-Bloc Mini-Creta, Allan Block), armour stone, and natural stone walls. Walls over 1 metre are engineered to Ontario Building Code with full geogrid reinforcement and drainage chimneys.
What areas do you serve?
Hamilton, Burlington, Oakville, Waterdown, Ancaster, Dundas, Stoney Creek, Milton and surrounding Halton and Hamilton-area neighbourhoods.
Can I send photos before booking a quote?
Yes — there's an optional photo upload on the form. Send wide shots plus close-ups of any problem spots and we'll scope the project before the on-site visit.
Do you charge for the on-site interlock quote?
No. Every on-site interlock or stonework quote is free with no obligation. We measure, check subgrade and access, and walk you through Unilock and Techo-Bloc options on the spot. Written itemized quote follows within 3 business days.
How soon can you start an interlock project?
Peak hardscape season (May–August) typically books 6–10 weeks out across Hamilton, Burlington, and Oakville. Front walkways and small entrances can sometimes slot in within 3 weeks. Fall installs (September–October) are faster to book and a great time to lock in pricing.
Do you remove the existing patio, driveway or sod before installing?
Yes. Demo and disposal of the old surface (sod, asphalt, concrete, or old pavers) are line items on every quote. The base is then excavated to 10–12 inches, geotextile is laid, and a fresh compacted granular base is built before any pavers go down.
Do you require a deposit on an interlock job?
Yes — a deposit secures your install date and covers Unilock or Techo-Bloc material orders, which often have lead times of 1–3 weeks. Balance is split between mid-project and final walkthrough. All terms are written on the quote.
What is your interlock workmanship warranty?
Five-year written workmanship warranty on every interlock project covering settlement, edge failure, joint loss and base-related issues. Manufacturer warranty on Unilock and Techo-Bloc pavers themselves runs separately (typically lifetime transferable on the structural unit).
Can I see Unilock or Techo-Bloc samples before I commit?
Yes — we bring sample boards to every on-site visit so you can see Unilock series like Beacon Hill, Richcliff and Series 3000, plus Techo-Bloc lines like Blu, Aberdeen and Industria, against your home's brick and trim. Picking the paver in person beats picking from a photo every time.
