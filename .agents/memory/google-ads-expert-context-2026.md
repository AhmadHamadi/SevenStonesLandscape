# Google Ads Expert Context - May 2026

Use this file as standing context whenever the user asks about Google Ads, paid search, paid lead generation, contractor advertising, LSAs, landing pages, tracking, or campaign strategy.

## Operating Posture

- Treat Google Ads in 2026 as an AI-assisted acquisition system, not a keyword-only media buy.
- Start with business economics: revenue per job, close rate, gross margin, service area, capacity, seasonality, and acceptable cost per booked job.
- Prioritize lead quality over raw lead volume. Contractor accounts fail when bidding optimizes toward junk calls, unqualified forms, low-intent clicks, or misclassified conversions.
- Never trust default recommendations blindly. Auto-apply settings, broad match, Performance Max, AI Max, final URL expansion, auto-created assets, and lead form assets must be reviewed against the offer, service area, and compliance risk.
- Build the account around closed-loop measurement: click or call -> lead -> qualified lead -> estimate -> booked job -> revenue.

## 2026 Google Ads Landscape

- Smart Bidding remains central. Google defines Smart Bidding as auction-time bidding for conversion or conversion value goals, including Maximize conversions, Target CPA, Maximize conversion value, and Target ROAS.
- Broad match works best when paired with Smart Bidding and clean conversion data. Without strong negatives and real lead-quality feedback, it can expand into low-quality contractor queries.
- AI Max for Search campaigns is a beta-era Search enhancement, not a replacement for strategy. It can expand matching, customize text/assets, and improve reporting, but it needs strict brand, offer, location, and landing-page controls.
- Performance Max can work for local service businesses only when it receives strong inputs: accurate conversion goals, negative keywords/brand exclusions where available, clear asset groups, service-area alignment, real creative, and offline lead-quality data.
- Search campaigns are still the contractor workhorse for high-intent services because they provide clearer query control than PMax.
- Local Services Ads are often essential for home services because they appear in prominent local placements and charge per lead rather than per click, but they are governed by separate profile, verification, responsiveness, review, and lead-quality mechanics.

## Best Strategic Stack For Contractors

1. Google Business Profile and Local Services Ads for near-me/high-trust demand.
2. High-intent Search campaigns for emergency, repair, installation, replacement, and city/service queries.
3. Remarketing and YouTube/Display only when conversion volume, creative, and consent/tracking are mature enough.
4. Performance Max only after Search and LSAs have reliable conversion data, or when a separate experimental budget exists.
5. CRM/offline conversion imports as soon as lead volume exists. Import qualified leads and converted/booked jobs, not only form fills.
