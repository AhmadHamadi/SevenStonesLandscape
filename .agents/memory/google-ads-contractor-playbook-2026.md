# Google Ads Contractor Playbook - May 2026

Use this as the default playbook for contractors: roofing, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, renovation, landscaping, masonry, paving, waterproofing, cleaning, restoration, garage door, locksmith, and other local service businesses.

## Account Structure

- Separate brand, emergency/urgent services, core non-emergency services, high-ticket installs, and competitor campaigns when budget allows.
- Segment by economics, not vanity categories. Example: "emergency plumbing" should not share budgets and bidding with "bathroom renovation ideas."
- For small budgets, start with one tight Search campaign per high-value service cluster and one LSA profile if eligible.
- Use location targeting conservatively: target only cities/service areas the contractor can actually serve profitably. Exclude weak areas if drive time, permit requirements, job size, or close rates are poor.
- Use ad schedules that match answer capacity. For contractors, missed calls can damage both performance and LSA responsiveness.

## Keyword Strategy

- Start with phrase/exact for expensive emergency categories, then test broad match only with Smart Bidding, strong negatives, and enough conversion data.
- Prioritize bottom-funnel modifiers: emergency, near me, open now, repair, replacement, installation, contractor, company, quote, estimate, licensed, insured, city names.
- Separate research/informational traffic unless the landing page and budget are designed for nurture.
- Build negative lists for DIY, jobs/careers, salary, training, wholesale, parts-only, free, cheap if not aligned, reviews-only, templates, how-to, used equipment, and irrelevant industries.
- Check search terms frequently during launch and after any automation change.

## Ads And Offers

- Ads should promise the next action, not generic greatness. Use clear service, city/area, urgency, proof, and call-to-action.
- Strong contractor proof points: licensed, insured, local, years in business, warranty, financing, same-day availability, emergency availability, certified installers, real reviews, before/after work, manufacturer partnerships.
- Avoid unsupported claims like "best," "guaranteed cheapest," or claims that cannot be proven on the landing page.
- Use call assets, location assets, sitelinks, structured snippets, callouts, image assets, and lead form assets only when they match the campaign goal and tracking plan.

## Landing Page Requirements

- Match one page to one service intent whenever possible.
- Above the fold: service + city/service area, phone number, quote form, trust proof, urgency, and a clear promise.
- Include license/insurance signals, service-area map or city list, reviews, job photos, FAQs, financing/warranty where applicable, and a no-friction contact path.
- Forms should capture enough data to qualify: name, phone, email, service, city/postal code, urgency, short job description, optional photo upload.
- For emergency services, phone-first design usually beats long forms.

## Budget And Bidding

- Launch with enough budget to learn. Tiny daily budgets in expensive categories usually create noisy data.
- Use Maximize conversions first when conversion volume is low, then add Target CPA after stable lead volume. Move toward value-based bidding only after qualified/converted lead values are imported reliably.
- Do not optimize only to calls over a minimum duration if many bad calls are long. Import qualified calls or booked jobs when possible.
- Give major bid strategy changes at least 1-2 weeks unless spend is clearly wasteful or tracking is broken.

## Contractor KPI Hierarchy

1. Cost per booked job.
2. Cost per qualified lead.
3. Close rate by campaign/query/service.
4. Revenue and gross margin by campaign.
5. Lead response time and missed-call rate.
6. Search term quality.
7. Cost per raw lead.
8. CTR/CPC/impressions, used as diagnostics only.

## Contractor Red Flags

- Lots of calls, few booked jobs.
- Forms missing phone/email, making enhanced conversions and follow-up weak.
- Broad match or PMax running without negatives or offline lead feedback.
- "Near me" traffic outside real service territory.
- LSA profile has few reviews, low rating, poor photos, stale hours, or unanswered calls.
- Campaigns optimize to every form/call equally, even spam, vendors, job seekers, and wrong-service leads.
