Our Story

Rooted in Hamilton, Built for Ontario

Seven Stones Landscape started in Hamilton with a simple idea: homeowners deserve outdoor spaces that are built right — not just pretty on day one, but still solid after a decade of Ontario freeze-thaw and clay soil. We've seen too many patios heave and walls bow because the base or drainage was short-changed. So we committed to doing it the other way.

Our team grew from local projects along the Niagara Escarpment and across the Golden Horseshoe — from Ancaster and Dundas to Burlington, Oakville, and beyond. We source hardscape materials from trusted suppliers along the QEW corridor and, when the design calls for it, we work with local nurseries in the Hamilton–Halton region so plantings are suited to our zone. This isn't generic landscaping — it's hardscaping and landscaping built for Hamilton's clay-heavy soil, Halton's codes, and the weather that tests every base we lay.

The First Project

From One Backyard to Hundreds

The first project we completed in Hamilton was a modest interlock patio and walkway for a family who wanted a place for their kids to play and for weekend get-togethers. We excavated, put down a proper granular base — no skimping — and laid the stone. That was over 12 years ago. The patio is still level, and that family has since had us back for a retaining wall and more. That job set the standard: drainage and base first, then the finish. We've carried that same discipline into every project since, from the Escarpment to the lake.

Real-World Proof

The Experience Behind Every Project

We don't just install patios — we engineer them for Ontario's conditions. With 12+ years of hands-on work across the Golden Horseshoe, we've learned that clay-heavy soil in Hamilton, Ancaster, and Stoney Creek demands a deeper, well-compacted base and careful drainage. We're ICPI certified for concrete paver installation and we work regularly with Techo-Bloc and Unilock products — the same materials you see at local suppliers and in award-winning projects. Our crews use excavators, laser levels, and compaction equipment so every base is precise and every surface drains away from your home. That combination of certification, materials, and tooling is what separates a patio that lasts from one that shifts in a few winters.

How We Work

Drainage-First Engineering & Sustainable Choices

Drainage isn't optional — it's the foundation. Every patio, walkway, and retaining wall we build is designed so water moves away from your house and never pools under the base. We slope surfaces, use proper aggregate and weep holes where needed, and in heavy clay areas we build bases that can handle the swell and shrink of Ontario seasons. That's drainage-first engineering: it's what keeps your investment from heaving or cracking.

When it comes to softscape and plantings, we favour sustainable, local options where the design allows — species that thrive in our zone and require less water and chemicals. We're not purists; we're pragmatists. The goal is a landscape that looks great, performs for decades, and doesn't fight the environment we work in.
