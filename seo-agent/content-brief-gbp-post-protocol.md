# Content Brief, Blog, And GBP Post Protocol

Use this protocol when the audit needs blog topics, page copy direction, service-page descriptions, GBP posts, GBP service descriptions, or content refresh recommendations.

## Core Principle

Good SEO content should help a real customer make a decision. It should not look like generic AI filler, scraped competitor copy, keyword stuffing, or scaled content made only for rankings.

## Google Search Baseline

Use Google's people-first content guidance and spam policies as the baseline:

- Content should provide original information, real experience, useful detail, and substantial value compared with other search results.
- SEO should help search engines and users understand useful content, not replace usefulness.
- Avoid scaled content abuse, doorway pages, copied competitor wording, mass-produced thin articles, and content created only to manipulate rankings.
- Do not write to an arbitrary word count. Depth should match intent and usefulness.

## Blog Keyword Selection

Before recommending a blog topic, classify the query:

| Intent | Best Page Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hire/book/quote/near me/service + city | Service page or local landing page | Do not let a blog steal the money-page target. |
| How/what/why/cost/process/problem | Blog, guide, FAQ, or support section | Use the blog to support service pages with internal links. |
| Best/top/comparison/alternative | Comparison page, service guide, or blog depending on buyer stage | Must be fair, specific, and evidence-backed. |
| Brand or competitor | Brand page, comparison page, or reputation content | Do not make misleading competitor claims. |
| Existing customer support | Help article or FAQ | Usually lower SEO priority unless it supports retention or conversions. |

## New Content Cannibalization Preflight

Run this before recommending or writing a new blog or page:

1. Identify the primary intent.
2. Search the existing site inventory for pages with the same intent, title pattern, H1, or internal anchors.
3. Decide whether the new content should be:
   - A new page
   - A section added to an existing page
   - A refresh/merge of an existing page
   - A supporting blog that internally links to a service page
   - Rejected because it would cannibalize or duplicate existing content
4. Assign one owner URL for the primary keyword.
5. Define the internal link target and anchor text before publishing.

## Competitor-Informed Content

Use competitors to learn what the SERP rewards, not to copy:

- Extract competitor titles, H1s, H2s, FAQs, trust proof, schema, media, CTAs, and local proof.
- Identify what they cover that we genuinely should cover.
- Identify what they fail to explain that we can answer better.
- Add original proof: project examples, photos, process, pricing ranges, timelines, local details, review themes, team expertise, before/after details, or case studies.
- Do not copy wording, paragraph order, claims, business descriptions, review language, or FAQ phrasing.

## No AI-Slop Content Gate

Reject or rewrite content that has these signs:

- Generic intro that could fit any business.
- No first-hand details, local proof, project examples, photos, or service specifics.
- Repeats the keyword unnaturally.
- Uses vague claims like "high quality service" without proof.
- Summarizes competitors without adding original value.
- Uses filler sections that do not answer buyer questions.
- Makes unsupported guarantees, fake statistics, fake reviews, fake certifications, or fake awards.
- Has a title that overpromises or does not match the page.
- Reads like a template with swapped city/service names.

## GBP Post Rules

- Keep posts useful, timely, and customer-facing.
- Use a real photo or video when possible.
- Use one clear CTA.
- Match the landing page to the post topic.
- Do not stuff cities or services into every sentence.
- Do not post fake promotions, fake urgency, fake reviews, or unsupported claims.
- Do not treat GBP posts as a replacement for complete categories, services, reviews, photos, and website relevance.

## GBP Service Description Rules

- Describe the service in plain customer language.
- Include the service area only when it is true and useful.
- Mention differentiators that can be proven.
- Avoid repeating the same keyword across every service description.
- Match GBP services with website content where possible.
