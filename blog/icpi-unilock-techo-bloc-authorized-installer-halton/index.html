ICPI, Unilock & Techo-Bloc Authorized Installer in Halton: What the Credentials Actually Mean

By Riaad, Owner & Project Manager · ICPI Certified Installer · Last updated May 20, 2026

Three credentials matter when hiring a paver contractor in Halton: ICPI Certified Installer (industry installation standard), Unilock Authorized Contractor (Unilock product warranty), and Techo-Bloc Authorized Contractor (Techo-Bloc product warranty). Each one is independently verifiable through the issuing organization's online directory. As of 2026, fewer than 15 contractors across Hamilton, Burlington, Oakville, Milton, and Mississauga carry all three simultaneously. Seven Stones Landscape is one of them, certified since 2013.

Why credentials matter more in Halton than most markets

Halton-Till clay soil, 60+ freeze-thaw cycles per Hamilton winter, and road-salt exposure together create one of the harshest paver environments in North America. Installation errors that go unnoticed in a milder climate fail in 3 to 5 years here. The ICPI / Unilock / Techo-Bloc credential stack is the homeowner's only third-party-verifiable way to know an installer has been trained on the specs that survive this climate.

ICPI Certified Installer: the industry standard

The Interlocking Concrete Pavement Institute is the North American trade association for paver installation. ICPI Certified Installer is the credential earned through:

  • Classroom training on subgrade prep, base aggregate, edge restraint, joint sand, and pattern integrity
  • Written exam on ICPI Tech Spec 2 (Construction of Interlocking Concrete Pavement), Tech Spec 4 (Structural Design of Vehicular ICP), and Tech Spec 17 (Permeable ICP)
  • Documented field experience (minimum project portfolio)
  • Continuing education every 3 years to maintain certification

Verify any contractor's status at icpi.org by searching the company name. If the contractor is not in the directory, they are not currently ICPI certified.

Unilock Authorized Contractor: warranty coverage on Unilock pavers

Unilock runs a multi-tier installer program: Authorized, Premium, and Elite. Authorized Contractor is the entry credential and requires:

  • Completion of Unilock's installation training
  • Agreement to install per Unilock's published spec sheet
  • Portfolio review
  • Annual recertification

The benefit to homeowners is direct: Unilock's Lifetime Guarantee on the pavers stays valid only when installed by an Authorized Contractor (or higher tier). Pavers installed by a non-authorized contractor carry no manufacturer warranty even if the product is genuine Unilock.

Verify any contractor at contractor.unilock.com or the public-facing unilock.com/find-contractor.

Techo-Bloc Authorized Contractor: warranty coverage on Techo-Bloc pavers

Techo-Bloc's program tiers run Pro, Authorized, and Elite. Pro is baseline and requires installation training plus reference projects. Authorized adds annual training and a minimum project-volume threshold. Elite is invitation-only and requires top-tier portfolio plus verified customer reviews. As with Unilock, the Techo-Bloc Lifetime Guarantee on pavers stays valid only when installed by Authorized or Elite contractors.

Verify any contractor at techo-bloc.com/landscape-contractor.

What else to check beyond the three credentials

  • Landscape Ontario Horticultural Trades Association membership — provincial credential covering landscape, hardscape, lighting, sod, design-build. Verify at landscapeontario.com.
  • WSIB Clearance Certificate — confirms workers compensation is current; protects the homeowner from liability if a worker is injured on site.
  • General liability insurance — $2M minimum, ideally $5M. Ask for the actual certificate of insurance, not a verbal claim.
  • Workmanship warranty — separate from the manufacturer paver warranty; covers the contractor's installation labour. 5 years is the residential industry standard.
  • NCMA (National Concrete Masonry Association) — relevant credential for segmental retaining walls (Allan Block, Unilock Pisa2, Techo-Bloc Mini-Creta).
  • BBB accreditation + Google Business Profile review history — independent reputation signals, separate from manufacturer credentials.

How to verify any Halton contractor in 5 minutes

  1. ICPI: icpi.org → Find a Pro / Member Directory → search company name.
  2. Unilock: unilock.com/find-contractor → enter postal code → check Authorized tier.
  3. Techo-Bloc: techo-bloc.com/landscape-contractor → search city → check Authorized or Elite badge.
  4. Landscape Ontario: landscapeontario.com → Member Directory.
  5. BBB Canada: bbb.org/ca → search business name + city.

If a contractor refuses to give the exact company name registered with each directory or provides a name that does not appear in the search, treat that as a strong red flag. Credentials that cannot be verified independently are not credentials.
