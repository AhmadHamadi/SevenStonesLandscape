Interlock Driveway Cost in Hamilton, Burlington and Oakville: 2026 Pricing Guide

March 26, 2026|By Seven Stones Landscape

Interlock driveways are one of the highest-intent projects homeowners ask us about, especially in Hamilton, Burlington, and Oakville where curb appeal and winter durability both matter. The first question is almost always cost. The honest answer is that pricing changes with square footage, access, base requirements, edge detail, and paver selection. This guide explains the main pricing variables so you can compare quotes more confidently.

In this article

  • Quick answer
  • What changes the price most
  • Why base depth matters in Ontario
  • Material and design choices
  • Repair vs full replacement
  • How to compare quotes

Quick answer

Interlock driveway cost in 2026 depends less on the word "driveway" and more on the conditions underneath it. A small, straightforward driveway with clean access is a different job from a wider front-yard rebuild with deep excavation, drainage correction, and premium pavers. That is why serious pricing starts with an on-site review, not a generic square-foot number alone.

What changes interlock driveway pricing the most?

  • Square footage and layout complexity
  • Removal of the existing surface
  • Excavation depth and base-build requirements
  • Drainage corrections and edge restraint details
  • Paver line, border style, pattern, and color mix
  • Access constraints for equipment and disposal

That is why two driveways on the same street can price very differently. One may only need a clean rebuild, while the other also needs drainage work, curbside transitions, or design upgrades tied to a front-yard project.

Why base depth matters in Hamilton, Burlington, and Oakville

Ontario winters reward proper preparation and punish shortcuts. In clay-heavy areas, shallow base work is one of the fastest ways to end up with heaving, settlement, and repeated repair bills. A driveway is a structural hardscape surface, so base depth, compaction, and drainage matter more than the paver color on top.

This is one reason homeowners comparing interlock patios and driveways should ask what is included below grade. If the quote does not explain excavation, compaction, edge restraint, and drainage, it is not giving you the full performance picture.

How much do materials and design affect the quote?

Material selection can shift pricing quickly. Standard-format pavers usually cost less than premium large-format or heavily textured products. Borders, soldier courses, inlays, and custom banding also add labor and layout time. Those upgrades can be worth it for curb appeal, especially in neighborhoods where the driveway is a major part of the front elevation, but they should be chosen intentionally.

If you are also weighing concrete, our comparison of stamped concrete vs interlock in Ontario can help clarify the tradeoffs.

Should you repair or replace an existing interlock driveway?

If the issue is localized, a targeted lift-and-reset can be the right move. If settlement, drainage failure, or widespread movement runs through the whole surface, a full rebuild often makes more financial sense over time. That is especially true when the driveway ties into front steps, walkways, or retaining features that have also shifted.

Homeowners who want to understand the repair side can also review our interlock repair solution page.

How to compare interlock driveway quotes properly

When you compare proposals, ask for these details in writing:

  • Excavation scope and disposal
  • Base depth and compaction method
  • Drainage slope and water-management details
  • Paver brand or line
  • Border, edge restraint, and transition scope
  • Any permit or driveway-approval considerations if applicable

That checklist usually tells you far more than a headline price. It also helps separate a durable driveway build from a quote that only looks competitive because key structural items are missing.

Bottom line

Interlock driveway pricing in Hamilton, Burlington, and Oakville depends on the full construction scope, not just the visible stone. If you want a realistic number, the right next step is an on-site review that accounts for base depth, drainage, access, and material choices.
